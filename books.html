Books

Holding Fast: Latino Immigrant Civic Engagement and Resilience in Polarizing Times

With James McCann · Russell Sage Foundation Press · 2020 · 162 pp.

Drawing on the 2012 and 2016 Latino Immigrant National Election Studies, this book examines how Latino immigrants maintained — and in some cases deepened — their civic engagement in the face of escalating political hostility. Winner, Best Book in Latino Politics, Latino/a Caucus, APSA, 2021.

Outsiders No More? Models of Immigrant Political Incorporation

Edited with Jennifer Hochschild, Claudine Gay, and Jacqueline Chattopadhyay · Oxford University Press · 2013 · 368 pp.

A comparative volume examining how immigrants become political actors across different national contexts. Contributors assess variation in citizenship regimes, party systems, and civil society structures to develop new frameworks for understanding incorporation.

Publisher page →

Latinos in the New Millennium: An Almanac of Opinion, Behavior, and Policy Preferences

With Luis Fraga, John Garcia, Rodney Hero, Valerie Martinez-Ebers, and Gary Segura · Cambridge University Press · 2012 · 448 pp.

Drawing on the landmark 2006 Latino National Survey — the most comprehensive national survey of Latinos ever conducted — this volume documents Latino political opinion, civic participation, identity, and policy preferences across the United States.

Publisher page →

Latino Lives in America: Making It Home

With Luis Fraga, John Garcia, Rodney Hero, Valerie Martinez-Ebers, and Gary Segura · Temple University Press · 2010 · 224 pp.

A sweeping analysis of Latino political life in America — from immigration and naturalization to electoral participation and policy preferences — grounded in the Latino National Survey. Excerpted in The New Latino Politics Reader (University of California Press, 2016).

Publisher page →

Governing American Cities: Inter-Ethnic Coalitions, Competition, and Conflict

Edited volume · Russell Sage Foundation · 2001 · 224 pp.

An edited collection examining the politics of multi-ethnic cities, with essays on coalition formation, competition for resources, and the governance challenges posed by increasing urban diversity in American metropolitan areas.

Publisher page →

Between Two Nations: The Political Predicament of Latinos in New York City

Cornell University Press · 1998 · 272 pp.

Explores why Latinos in New York have been slow to naturalize and participate in electoral politics, tracing the political orientations of Dominican and Colombian immigrants across two nations. Winner, Best Book on Ethnic Political Incorporation, Race and Ethnic Politics Section, APSA, 1999. Excerpted in Urban Politics Reader (Routledge, 2007) and The Restless City Reader (Routledge, 2010).
