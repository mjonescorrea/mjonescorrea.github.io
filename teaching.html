Teaching

I teach courses at both the undergraduate and graduate levels in American politics, immigration, race and ethnicity, and urban politics. My courses combine close reading of primary research with broader questions about democracy, civic life, and political inequality.

Undergraduate Courses — University of Pennsylvania

PoliSci 1103

Dilemmas of Immigration

An examination of immigration as a political and social phenomenon, covering the policies that shape the movement of people, the experiences of immigrants upon arrival, and debates over enforcement, integration, and belonging in American political life. Offered spring 2018, 2020, 2022, 2023; fall 2025.

PoliSci 4491

Philadelphia: Power, Space and Diversity

A study of how race, class, immigration, and urban geography shape political power and civic life in Philadelphia. Combines political science frameworks with on-the-ground engagement with the city. Offered fall 2018, 2020, 2021, 2023, 2024.

PoliSci 220

The Politics of Slow-Moving Crises

An exploration of political challenges that unfold gradually — including economic inequality, demographic change, and environmental degradation — and why democratic institutions often struggle to respond to them. Offered spring 2019, 2021.

PoliSci 398

Immigration Policy and Immigrant Politics

An advanced undergraduate seminar on U.S. immigration policy and its political consequences, covering the history of immigration law, contemporary enforcement debates, and the political behavior of immigrant communities.

Graduate Courses — University of Pennsylvania

PoliSci 598

Politics, Groups and Identities

A doctoral seminar on theories of group identity, ethnic and racial politics, and political behavior, with attention to methodological debates and the intersection of race, class, and immigration in American political life.

PoliSci 599

Political Behavior / Power, Space and Diversity

Graduate survey of core theories and evidence on political participation, public opinion, and civic engagement, with special attention to variation across race, class, and immigrant status.

PoliSci 798

Comparative Politics of Immigration

A doctoral seminar examining immigration as a comparative political phenomenon, covering variation across national citizenship regimes, sub-national policy environments, and party systems in shaping immigrant political incorporation.

PoliSci 799

Emerging Frontiers in Latinx Politics

A doctoral seminar on canonical and cutting-edge scholarship in Latino and Latinx politics, with emphasis on methodological approaches, ongoing empirical debates, and the development of research agendas.
